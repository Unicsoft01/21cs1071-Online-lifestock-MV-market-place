1.2       Statements of Problem

An attempt he traditional livestock trading process, which relies heavily on physical marketplaces, presents several significant challenges for both traders and buyers. These challenges include:

1.      Limited Market Access: Traders and buyers are often restricted to local markets, which limits the scope of potential transactions.

2.      High Transaction Costs: The need for physical meetings between traders and buyers incurs costs related to transportation, accommodation, and time, which can be prohibitively expensive, especially for those in rural or remote areas.

3.      Inefficient Communication: Traditional livestock markets often suffer from poor communication channels, leading to delays in negotiations and misunderstandings between parties.

4.      Security and Safety Concerns: Conducting transactions in physical markets poses security risks, including theft and fraud.
