1.1       Background of the Study

Todayâ€™s Livestock trading is a critical component of the agricultural economy, particularly in regions where livestock farming is a primary source of income. Traditionally, this trade has relied heavily on physical marketplaces where buyers and sellers meet in person to negotiate prices and finalize transactions. However, these traditional methods are fraught with challenges, including high transaction costs, limited market access, and inefficiencies in communication and logistics Mwangi et al., (2020).

The advent of digital technology has brought significant changes to various sectors, including agriculture. E-commerce platforms have revolutionized how goods and services are traded, offering convenience, efficiency, and broader market reach Okello, (2019). However, the livestock industry has been slow to adopt these digital innovations. The lack of specialized platforms tailored to the unique needs of livestock trading has been a major barrier to the digital transformation of this sector Nakamura & Nkamnebe, (2021).
